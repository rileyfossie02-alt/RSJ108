How to Conduct a Strong Interview

Assembly time: 20–30 minutes
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Interviewing is one of the most important skills in journalism. Knowing how to ask strong questions and listen effectively helps you gather meaningful information for stories, podcasts, and videos.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Notebook or notes app
  • Pen or pencil
  • Phone or recording device
  • Prepared questions
  • Quiet space or stable internet connection

  1. Research your interview subject.

    Learn background information so you can ask informed questions.

    Research helps you ask better questions

  2. Prepare open-ended questions.

    Write questions that encourage detailed answers.

    Open-ended questions create better conversations

  3. Choose a good interview setting.

    Pick a quiet and professional environment.

    A good setting improves focus

  4. Introduce yourself and explain the purpose.

    Be clear about who you are and why you're interviewing them.

    A strong introduction builds trust

  5. Ask questions clearly and confidently.

    Speak clearly and allow time for responses.

    Confidence improves communication

  6. Listen actively and take notes.

    Record or write down key information.

    Taking notes helps capture details

  7. Ask follow-up questions.

    Go deeper into important topics.

    Follow-ups create stronger stories

  8. Stay professional and respectful.

    Maintain a respectful tone throughout the interview.

    Respect builds trust

  9. End and review.

    Thank your subject and review your notes.
