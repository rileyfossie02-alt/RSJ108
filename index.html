How to Conduct a Strong Interview

Assembly time: 20–30 minutes
Awesome rating: 95% awesome

Interviewing is one of the most important skills in journalism. Whether you're writing a story, creating a podcast, or filming a video, knowing how to ask the right questions and listen effectively will help you get meaningful information. Follow these steps to conduct a successful interview.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Notebook or notes app
  • Pen or pencil
  • Phone or recording device
  • Prepared questions
  • Quiet space or stable internet connection

  1. Research your interview subject.

    Learn basic background information so you can ask informed and relevant questions.

  2. Prepare open-ended questions.

    Write 5–10 questions that encourage detailed responses instead of yes/no answers.

  3. Choose a good interview setting.

    Pick a quiet location or ensure strong internet connection for virtual interviews.

  4. Introduce yourself and explain the purpose.

    Be clear about who you are and what the interview will be used for.

  5. Ask questions clearly and confidently.

    Speak clearly and give your interviewee time to fully answer.

  6. Listen actively and take notes.

    Record the interview if possible (with permission) and write down key points.

  7. Ask follow-up questions.

    Dig deeper when something interesting or important comes up.

  8. Stay professional and respectful.

    Maintain a positive tone and avoid interrupting your subject.

  9. End and review.

    Thank the interviewee and review your notes to identify key quotes.
